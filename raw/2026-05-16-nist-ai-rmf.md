# NIST AI Risk Management Framework Summary

## Framework Overview

NIST's AI RMF represents a collaborative effort to address AI-related risks. According to the page, it aims to "improve the ability to incorporate trustworthiness considerations into the design, development, use, and evaluation of AI products, services, and systems."

Released January 26, 2023, the framework emerged through a "consensus-driven, open, transparent, and collaborative process" involving public comment periods, workshops, and stakeholder input.

## Core Functions

The framework employs four primary functions represented visually in the circular design:
- **Govern**
- **Map**
- **Measure**
- **Manage**

## Supporting Resources

NIST published complementary materials including:
- An AI RMF Playbook
- A roadmap document
- Crosswalk guidance
- Perspectives and use cases via the Trustworthy and Responsible AI Resource Center (launched March 30, 2023)

## Generative AI Profile

Released July 26, 2024, **NIST-AI-600-1** specifically addresses generative AI risks. This profile "can help organizations identify unique risks posed by generative AI" and proposes corresponding risk management strategies.

## Critical Infrastructure Initiative

An April 7, 2026 concept note outlined an AI RMF Profile for critical infrastructure operators, focusing on trustworthy AI implementation within that sector.
